Spend your money more effectively by understanding your past spending

Build a deep understanding of your past spending by getting answers to these questions:

  • what is my spending overall?
  • how does my more recent spending compare to what I have spent in the past?
  • where does the brunt of my money go?
  • where should I make adjustments to my spending?
  • in what categories is my spending predictable?
  • in what categories is it less predictable?
  • others?

Get the answers to these questions by creating a file containing your spending transactions and uploading it here - after registering (see below).

If you use Mint (mint.com), use the downloaded file from the transactions section of the site.

The file must contain:

  • a first row of column headings containing these headers:
    • 'Date' - the date of the transaction
    • 'Category' - the name of the group containing similar transactions
    • 'Transaction Type' - 'debit' = money out or 'credit' = money in
    • 'Amount' - how much of the debit or credit
    • (these column headings come automatically with the Mint download)
  • (there can be any number of other columns in the file)
  • only transactions populating the remaining rows in the file
